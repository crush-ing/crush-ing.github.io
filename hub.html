You must be 18+ to commission me.

What I Will Draw


• Original characters
• Fanart & fan characters
• Any species - animals, humans, monsters, robots, etc.
• Varied body types or otherwise unique anatomy

Take a moment to browse my work - consider my style and what I typically draw. I don't like to set hard limits for myself creatively, and the list of things I would never draw under any circumstances (that I can think of at least) is pretty small and highly unlikely to be requested anyway, so I won't bother with specifics there. I do have preferences however and may turn down a commission if I don't feel inspired or confident enough in my ability to do the subject justice.

While I am primarily a character artist I'm open to other things such as pet portraits or experimental work. I would be happy to hear any and all ideas even if it turns out that I can't help you. Detailed backgrounds aren't currently within my skillset but I can do simple pattern or transparent backgrounds for inked illustrations.

Contact & Payment


These prices are an approximate range, the cost will ultimately depend on the complexity of the character design and scenario, how many drawings you want per page, the rendering, etc. If you have a budget I can tell you what I can do for that amount, or if you want something specific (ie. bust/fullbody, no color/color, shading, a sketchpage) I will quote you a price.

Payment is required up front and in full via PayPal invoice before I start working. I will send at least one WIP so you can ask for any changes or corrections before I finalize the piece.

Direct all inquiries to crushingcomms (AT) gmail (DOT) com. Be sure to attach all relevant reference images and information. Let me know if you would prefer your commission and/or name to remain private, otherwise it may be posted to my gallery sites or displayed on my website. You may repost your commission or use it for personal or commercial projects as long as I am credited as the artist. If you have any questions or need something clarified please don't hesitate to ask.

CRUSHING ✦ Art Musings

Why Digital Art?


I've been drawing since I could hold a pencil, but as a kid who was constantly moving, never had space to work or store things, and couldn't afford much supplies other than a #2 pencil and computer paper, it always felt like there was a limit to what I could do traditionally. Sometimes constraints force you to find creative solutions; other times they frustrate you to the point of giving up.

I did my fair share of doodling in MS Paint, but only became aware of digital art proper after joining DevaintArt in 2009 and seeing all the masterpieces people were creating with their computers. To me it seemed limitless in a way that traditional art simply wasn't. I never thought that going digital would make the process of drawing any easier, the appeal was more in the fact that with one compact piece of technology you could emulate any medium you wanted and easily share your work with thousands of people, some of whom might even be interested in it.

Programs


Around 2011 or so I managed to get my hands on a Wacom Bamboo Pen & Touch and a pirated version of Paint Tool SAI. Sadly I don't have any art from those early years because I deleted it all out of shame (just imagine poorly rendered drawings of Kagamine Len or something because that was probably the bulk of it). It took a while for me to find the right program and workflow since I don't enjoy the strict sketch > line > color method, but that's what all the cool kids did in the pretty anime art tutorials I tried following. I eventually broke away from that though and by 2016 I was doing weird shit like using the select tool for all of my lineart.

In 2018 I switched to a Wacom Intuos Draw and started using Krita because I fell in love with the sketch brush engine. Krita is great but I found it borderline unusable on my old low-end PC for anything more than low res drawing, so I was never able to fully commit to it. I ran the gauntlet of trying every other free program under the sun but only really liked Sketchbook enough to use it regularly. I eventually gave the Clip Studio Paint trial a shot, liked it a lot, bought the full version, and that has been my program of choice ever since.

Tools



I like brushes with high line width or opacity variation so I can get more mileage out of less tools. I also use little to no stabilization. For color selection I prefer HLS sliders since they're a bit more granular than your standard wheel, and I also have a color set with fully saturated colors for mixing directly on the canvas.

You can find all of the tools I use under Resources > Clip Studio Assets on the front page.

How I Learn


My earliest forays into art included drawing my dogs as ninjas and copying Pokémon card illustrations. Finding subjects and artists I really love and studying them is what keeps me motivated to create more than anything.

There are a lot of useless tutorials out there but a few nuggets of wisdom as well. This for example really spoke to the excessively technical part of my brain. I never did drill these specific exercises very much but I think the points about muscle memory and line control are always pertinent.

I'm fairly new to digital painting but most of what I know I learned from a guy called Borodante. I like how scientific his thought process is, once again appealing to the part of me that loves to obsesses over technicalities. He has a good beginner tutorial and his OVER-PAIN series is usually pretty informative.

CRUSHING ✦ Layout History

V1 - CRUSHING PETZ Is Born




CRUSHING began life in late 2020 as a Petz-only site hosted on Tumblr, since I found using Tumblr drafts for images and a private Discord server for downloads more convenient than whatever Neocities has going on.

I think this layout was edited from one of the templates on The Lilac Lynx, although heavily modified to the point that it doesn't resemble the original at all. I really owe Kalium for both getting me into Petz and kickstarting my web-making journey.


V2 - John Doe Saves The Day




Version 2 didn't change much aside from the navigation, which was a sticking point for me in the beginning since I didn't want to have to edit separate HTML files for every single page if they weren't going to have much content on them anyway. I was originally using JavaScript to make pages within a single HTML file, but that came with the caveat of not letting you link to individual pages (similar to but miraculously even worse than iFrames). I wasn't sure how to fix this until I stumbled across John Doe, which solved all of my problems with some extremely simple CSS.

I decided to move the site to Neocities after this update. As a funny aside, my Tumblr wound up suspended while I was trying to redirect to the new site because they apparently didn't like the script I was using.


V3 - Beautiful But Broken




Version 3 was my first attempt at building a more complex layout. The earlier iterations of the site were very bare-bones, which was fine by me since I valued the content and usability over anything else, but now that my navigation issues were solved and I understood more about HTML I wanted to experiment with the visuals. The result was nothing flashy, but I was satisfied with it. It did however produce a new problem in that it wasn't mobile responsive like the earlier layouts since they came with that out of the box, and it turned out to be inconsistent even across larger screen sizes which especially annoyed me.


V4 (Current) - GitHub Is Awesome


You are here!

After finishing the V3 layout I expanded the site to cover topics other than Petz, but continued using V2 for the new pages since it was more important to me that they were accesible on mobile. So if you were thinking V4 looks eerily similar to V2, that's because it is actually the same, I just removed the navbar and restructured the navigation using the dropdown menus from the V3 layout. I eventually moved the Petz site onto this layout as well after the poor responsiveness got to me.

This is also when I discovered GitHub Pages. I only had to test it out for a few days before I decided that moving to GitHub was the right call. The Neocities editor gets overloaded easily with my single HTML file method, navigating the folder structure on there is a nightmare, and they won't even host ZIPs which is why I had to use Discord for most of my file hosting needs. Using VS Code and GitHub Desktop to push updates and having everything hosted on the same server is just a massive upgrade by comparison.

Why Sumo?

Sumo first came to my attention in Fall of 2016. I was studying Japanese at the time and kept noticing sumo terms popping up in my vocab lists, so I looked it up out of curiosity and watched a few videos posted by a guy named Jason on YouTube.


Getting Hooked

The September tournament was underway so I followed the remaining few days via Jason's uploads and watched Goeido win his zensho. I was intrigued enough to watch the next few tournaments as well, and 2017 happened to open with Kisenosato being promoted to yokozuna and then fighting through the injury that ultimately ended his career. I think seeing his story play out in such a dramatic fashion is what really sold me on the sport, and I've been a fan ever since.
